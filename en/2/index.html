Biscuit Manual: Basics - Memorized Words

2014-06-17

This post will cover how you can manage your memorized words.   

You can access Memorized Words from the Boxes menu as shown below.





All flashcards that you swipe as memorized end up in this Box. Each flashcard that you send to Memorized Words increases your memorized word count tabs at the bottom. After 1 week, words will disappear from this list but still remain in your memorized word count totals. You can use the word count tabs to also view your memorized words by the last 7 days, last 30 days or all words you have memorized(total).   




You can restore a memorized word to its original Box by swiping it to the right. 



You can also completely delete a word by swiping to the left, but this will lower your memorized word count. 



Select any Box to leave the Memorized Words menu. You cannot add flashcards or access other settings/features from Memorized Words.  

Biscuit Manual: Data Transfer - Evernote

2014-06-17

This post will cover Biscuit’s functions for exporting to and importing from Evernote(https://evernote.com/).


Functions with Evernote can be accessed through the methods shown below. 




Link

You will need an Evernote account for these services to work with Biscuit, please check the url provided at the top. When you first use an Evernote function, you will need to link your Evernote account when prompted. Please enter your Evernote account information for this step, not your Biscuit account. Please note none of these Evernote functions will work when offline. 







After Link




Export

Exporting to Evernote creates a printer-friendly format for your Boxes and flashcards. Please press the sync button within the Evernote application if your Boxes do not appear right away.

When exporting to Evernote, a new notebook called Biscuit will appear and all of your Boxes will be within the notebook as separate notes. 
Each line of the note will be a flashcard. Each note has 3 columns  with the following format: word / part of speech / definition. 




Export to Evernote Peek

Evernote Peek is an iPad app that allows you to turn your iPad into an actual flashcard, please refer to this site for details: http://evernote.com/peek/.

Export to Evernote Peek only transfers your currently open Box; it will be exported as a new notebook. Please press the sync button within the Evernote application if it does not appear right away.

Each flashcard will be a note within the notebook matching the format necessary to use Evernote Peek: word as title and part of speech + definition within the body of the note. If you have downloaded audio pronunciations, a sound file for the word will be included within the body of the note.




Import

When you are importing from Evernote make sure to use notes within the Biscuit notebook. The title of the note will become the name of the Box created in Biscuit. To create or modify flashcards within your notes you have two options:
1) Simply type in one word per line with no spaces.  When imported, Biscuit will automatically fill in definitions that are available from your currently selected dictionary.
2) Create a two column table in your note. The first column will be words and you can fill in your own definitions in the second column. If the second column is left blank, a definition will not be filled in automatically. 
3) Create a three column table in your note. The first column will be words. The second column can be used to fill in parts of speech. The third column can be used to fill in your own definitions. If the second or third columns is left blank, the part of speech and definition will not be filled in automatically. 
*Tip: Any three of these methods can be combined within the same note you import. Just be sure to type in one word per line outside of the tables if you want to use method 1. 


Import Words from Image

Biscuit can also import words from an image placed within any note within the Biscuit notebook. The title of the note will become the name of the Box created in Biscuit. The words that are recognized(please note the process is not 100% accurate) will be made into flashcards with the definitions being automatically filled in based on the information available from your currently selected dictionary. 
*Tip: You can import from multiple pictures placed within the same note and also pictures can be combined with any of the three import methods in the above section even within the same note. 



Unlink

The Unlink button that appears will sign you out of your Evernote account and disconnect Evernote from Biscuit. You will need to link an Evernote account again to export and import in the future. 

Biscuit Manual: Data Transfer - Dropbox

2014-06-17

This post will cover Biscuit’s functions for exporting to and importing from Dropbox(https://www.dropbox.com/home). 


Functions with Dropbox can be accessed through the settings menu as shown in the pictures below. 




Link

You will need an account with Dropbox to connect with Biscuit, please check the url provided at the top. You will need to first link your Dropbox account; when prompted please sign in with your Dropbox account, not your Biscuit account.  




After Link




Export

When you export to Dropbox all of your Boxes will appear as text files in the Apps folder within a new folder named Biscuit. The entries within each file correspond to your Box in the following ways.
Each line is a flashcard from your Box. For each line the format is: word [tab] definition [tab] normal(0) / mark(1) / dim(2) . 




Import

When you are importing from Dropbox make sure to use a .txt file placed within the same folder that Dropxbox exports to, the Biscuit folder within the Apps folder. The name of the .txt file will become the name of the Box in Biscuit. To create or modify flashcards within your .txt files you have two options:
1) Only fill in the word portion with one word without any spaces per line. When imported, Biscuit will automatically fill in definitions that are available from your currently selected dictionary.
2) Create or modify flashcards using the following format for every line: word [tab] definition [tab] 1(for marked) / 2(for dimmed) / leave blank(for normal)


Unlink

The Unlink button that appears in the menu will sign you out of your Dropbox account and disconnect Dropbox from Biscuit. You will need to link a Dropbox account again to export and import in the future.
