Biscuit Manual: FAQ

2015-01-06

I don't remember my password.

Please press ‘Forgot Password?’ on the Sign In page. Biscuit will send you a temporary password via email. Please sign in with this password and change password immediately.

Please note that this temporary password will only be valid for 30 minutes.


A temporary password does not work.

In many cases, your email address is wrong. Biscuit send you a temporary even if there is no account of the email address. (for security)
Please try another email address to sign in.

Or you may have signed up with a email address with typo. Please send us email. We’ll find the correct email address you used.



Where is image to text?

We use the Evernote API to extract words from images. So we decide to provide the feature in a more proper way.

You must make Evernote notes including images instead of uploading a image in the Biscuit, and select ‘Import' from 'Settings/Data Transfer/Evernote’. Then Biscuit boxes with titles of Evernote notes and words of images will be created.

Biscuit Manual: Advanced Feature - Bake It(Android)

2014-08-13

This post will cover Biscuit's Bake It feature for easily looking up definitions and creating flashcards for anything you are reading on your device. 


To turn on Bake it functionality please go into the Settings menu as shown in the pictures below. 




Use the Bake it checkbox to turn Bake it on or off. To have Bake it run in the background, you only need to open the Biscuit app once.




When Bake it is running, you can switch to another app (such as Google Chrome) and highlight then copy a word to see its definition pop up at the top of your screen, and it will be added as a flashcard to the box that is open within the app. 



You can also tap the definition notification to go straight to the Biscuit app and see the flashcard that was just created. 


Notification View

Once you leave the app to use Bake it, you should get a notification letting you know that Bake it is running. 




The Biscuit icon will appear at the top of your screen letting you know Biscuit is running. Even if you turn off the app, Bake it will still run in the background. 




You can manage Bake it from the notification drawer as shown below. You can use the x box to turn Bake it off until you relaunch the app. 



If you would like to use Bake it without it showing up in your notification drawer, you can use the Hide notification view option. This will also remove the biscuit icon from the top bar.


Start Bake it Automatically

Turning on the last option in the Bake it menu will turn Bake it on whenever you turn on your phone. To turn Bake it off, you will need to either turn off Bake it from the notification drawer as described above or disable Bake it from the Bake it menu within the app. 

Biscuit Manual: Advanced Feature - Bake It(iOS)

2014-08-12

This post will cover Biscuit's Bake It feature for easily looking up definitions and creating flashcards for anything you are reading on your device. 


To turn on Bake it functionality please go into the Settings menu as shown in the pictures below. Use the switch to turn Bake it on or off.




When Bake it is on, you can switch to another app(such as Safari) and highlight then copy a word to see its definition pop up at the top of your screen, and it will be added as a flashcard to the box that is open within the app. 




You can also tap the definition notification to go straight to the Biscuit app and see the flashcard that was just created. 


Bake it Shut Off

Bake it will stop working if you close the Biscuit app. If you leave Biscuit running in the background for an extended period of time(about an hour) without using Bake it, the Bake it function will automatically shut off. If this happens, simply reopen the Biscuit app to have Bake it reactivated. 


Bake it Limitations

Bake it will not work in apps that block or limit the copy function. For example ebooks with DRM do not allow copy and paste therefore Biscuit will not be able to use Bake it when you view these ebooks.
