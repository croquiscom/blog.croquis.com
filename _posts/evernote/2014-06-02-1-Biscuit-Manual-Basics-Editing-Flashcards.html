This post will go into all the details for adding and modifying flashcards within Biscuit. 


Adding Flashcards

Swipe downwards(pull down) or tap on the black area at the top to create a flashcard(flashcards can also be added through Bake it, please see the Bake it(iOS) or Bake it(Android) post). 



OR




Type in the English word you would like to add and if it is in our dictionary, the definition should automatically appear right below. You will see the definition of the word update, based on entries within our dictionary, as you type each letter. Tap done on the keyboard to add the flashcard to the box.  


Modifying Definitions

When you are adding a flashcard, you can modify the definition by tapping on the definition space. If you would like to supply your own definition for a word that already has a definition, you can quickly erase the definition provided by simply adding a space to the end of the word. You can then click on the blank definition portion and type whatever you would like. 



When modifying the definition, you can add symbols for parts of speech (e.g. n, v, adj. ); the symbols appear right above your keyboard. You can clear all text from the definition field by swiping from right to left. 


Modified definitions will not autocomplete: After modifying a definition from our dictionary, changing the word portion of the flashcard will no longer cause the definition portion to change based on the closest entry in our dictionary. To restore the autocomplete functionality for that flashcard you must clear the word portion or the definition portion. 

When a flashcard is in editing mode, a lighter black/gray background is shown for definitions that have been modified.

Modifying Existing Flashcards 

After a flashcard has been added to the box, you can change its word or modify the definition by tapping on the letters of the word. Be careful however, if it is a definition you have not modified, the dictionary will automatically update the definition based on how the word is changed. This means that if you have switched languages since you saved the word, the definitions will appear in the new language. 

A word cannot be longer than the flashcard’s length shown on screen, however, there is no limit for the length of the definition portion. 


Gestures While Modifying Flashcards

- Swipe to the right on the word portion: This will bring up the next autocomplete suggestion in our dictionary along with its definition that use the letters you have already typed. To add any autocompleted word, you can simply press done instead of typing out all the letters.



- Swipe to the left on the word portion: This will clear the word along with the definition.

- Swipe to the left on the definition portion: This will clear the definition but leave the word intact making it easy to take your own notes for the word. 

- Tap anywhere below the definition and above the keyboard: This is the same as pushing the done button on your keyboard. If a new flashcard, this will add the flashcard to the box. If it is a flashcard you are editing, this will finish your editing session.
